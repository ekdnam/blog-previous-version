---
title: How do you find serendipity through your friends?
---
I have come to realize that much of life is shaped by forces beyond our control. Random events—both fortuitous and challenging—can steer us in unexpected directions. Yet, I believe we can tilt the odds in our favor. The key lies in deliberate action: actively engaging with the world rather than waiting for fortune to appear.

The idea is simple, almost trite: if you want to improve, you must work at it. Whether your goal is to enhance your communication skills, master a challenging subject, or understand emerging technologies, the solution is the same—immerse yourself in the process. Speak more to refine your thoughts; argue and debate to hone your reasoning; practice relentlessly to gain mastery.

Of course, action is often accompanied by fear—the fear of failure, of judgment, or of stepping into the unknown. For a long time, I allowed these fears to dictate my choices. Eventually, however, I discovered that life is too short to be paralyzed by what others might think. It is essential to act, even in the face of uncertainty. At the same time, action without reflection can lead us astray. That is why it is crucial to anchor our efforts in a set of guiding principles—principles that evolve as we learn more about the world.

Equally important is the willingness to seek and integrate feedback. Honest, constructive critique is invaluable; without it, our progress stalls. Not every piece of advice is beneficial—some come from voices that do not have our best interests at heart. Therefore, it is important to cultivate relationships where feedback is offered with genuine care, where the exchange is mutually uplifting rather than competitively zero-sum.

I have found that building such relationships resembles the process of peeling an onion: each conversation reveals new layers of insight. It is not about amassing connections, but about forging authentic bonds that enrich our lives and guide us through periods of uncertainty.

In short, while randomness is an inescapable part of our existence, we can influence our destiny by acting deliberately, seeking honest feedback, and nurturing meaningful relationships. By doing so, we transform chance into a form of self-made luck.
